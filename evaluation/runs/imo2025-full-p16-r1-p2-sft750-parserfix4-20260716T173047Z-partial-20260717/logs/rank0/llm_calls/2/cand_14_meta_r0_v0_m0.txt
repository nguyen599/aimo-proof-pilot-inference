## Problem
Let $\Omega$ and $\Gamma$ be circles with centres $M$ and $N$, respectively, such that the radius of $\Omega$ is less than the radius of $\Gamma$. Suppose $\Omega$ and $\Gamma$ intersect at two distinct points $A$ and $B$. Line $MN$ intersects $\Omega$ at $C$ and $\Gamma$ at $D$, so that $C, M, N, D$ lie on $MN$ in that order. Let $P$ be the circumcentre of triangle $ACD$. Line $AP$ meets $\Omega$ again at $E\neq A$ and meets $\Gamma$ again at $F\neq A$. Let $H$ be the orthocentre of triangle $PMN$.

Prove that the line through $H$ parallel to $AP$ is tangent to the circumcircle of triangle $BEF$.

## Solution
We prove the statement using analytic geometry.  The problem is invariant under similarity, so we may place the circles conveniently.

**1.  Coordinate set‑up.**  
Place the centre \(M\) of \(\Omega\) at \((0,0)\) and the centre \(N\) of \(\Gamma\) at \((1,0)\).  By scaling we may assume that \(\Omega\) is the unit circle: \(x^2+y^2=1\).  
Let the two intersection points be \(A=(a,b)\) with \(b>0\) and \(B=(a,-b)\).  Hence  
\[
a^2+b^2=1. \tag{1}
\]  
The centre \(N=(1,0)\) is the centre of \(\Gamma\), so  
\[
(a-1)^2+b^2 = R^2. \tag{2}
\]  
From (1) and (2) we obtain \(R^2=2-2a\).  Because the circles intersect in two distinct points we have \(|R-1|<1<R+1\); this gives \(a\in(-1,\tfrac12)\).  In particular \(R>1\) and \(a<\tfrac12\).

The line \(MN\) is the \(x\)-axis.  Its second intersection with \(\Omega\) is \(C=(-1,0)\) and with \(\Gamma\) is \(D=(1+R,0)\).  The order \(C,M,N,D\) on the \(x\)-axis is as required.

**2.  The point \(P\).**  
\(P\) is the circumcentre of \(\triangle ACD\).  Since \(C\) and \(D\) lie on the \(x\)-axis, the perpendicular bisector of \(CD\) is the vertical line \(x=\frac{-1+(1+R)}{2}=R/2\).  Write \(P=(X,Y)\) with \(X=R/2\).  From \(|P-C|=|P-A|\) we get  
\[
(X+1)^2+Y^2 = (X-a)^2+(Y-b)^2.
\]  
Substituting \(X=R/2\) and using (1) yields  
\[
Y = -\frac{(1+a)R}{2b}. \tag{3}
\]  
Thus  
\[
P=\Bigl(\frac R2,\;-\frac{(1+a)R}{2b}\Bigr).
\]

**3.  The line \(AP\).**  
The direction vector of \(AP\) is  
\[
\mathbf v = \Bigl(\frac R2-a,\; Y-b\Bigr).
\]  
Set  
\[
u = \frac R2-a,\qquad w = \frac{(1+a)u}{b}.
\]  
Then \(Y-b = -w\), so  
\[
\mathbf v = (u,\,-w). \tag{4}
\]  
The product \(S = u^2+w^2\) will be needed later.

**4.  The points \(E\) and \(F\).**  
The line \(AP\) is parametrised by \(z = A + t\mathbf v = (a+tu)+i(b-tw)\).  
*Intersection with \(\Omega\):*  \(|z|^2=1\) gives  
\[
t\bigl(2(au-bw)+tS\bigr)=0,
\]  
so the second intersection is \(E\) with \(t_E = -2(au-bw)/S\).

\[
t_E = \frac{2u}{S},\qquad
E = \Bigl(a+\frac{2u^2}{S},\; b-\frac{2uw}{S}\Bigr). \tag{5}
\]  
*Intersection with \(\Gamma\):*  \(\Gamma\) has centre \(N=(1,0)\) and radius \(R\).  The condition \(|z-1|^2=R^2\) gives  
\[
t\bigl(2((a-1)u-bw)+tS\bigr)=0,
\]  
and \( (a-1)u-bw = (a-1)u-(1+a)u = -2u\).  Thus  
\[
t_F = \frac{4u}{S},\qquad
F = \Bigl(a+\frac{4u^2}{S},\; b-\frac{4uw}{S}\Bigr). \tag{6}
\]  
Notice that \(t_F=2t_E\).

**5.  The orthocentre \(H\) of \(\triangle PMN\).**  
Since \(M=(0,0)\) and \(N=(1,0)\) lie on the \(x\)-axis, the altitude from \(P\) is the vertical line \(x=R/2\).  
The altitude from \(M\) is perpendicular to \(PN\).  Compute  
\[
PN = N-P = \Bigl(1-\frac R2,\; \frac{(1+a)R}{2b}\Bigr).
\]  
Its slope is \(\frac{(1+a)R}{2b(1-R/2)}\).  The altitude from \(M\) therefore has slope  
\(-\frac{2b(1-R/2)}{(1+a)R}\).  Its equation is \(y = m_M x\) with  
\(m_M = -\frac{2b(1-R/2)}{(1+a)R}\).  
Intersecting with \(x=R/2\) gives  
\[
H_y = m_M\cdot\frac R2 = -\frac{b(1-R/2)}{1+a}.
\]  
Thus  
\[
H = \Bigl(\frac R2,\; -\frac{b(1-R/2)}{1+a}\Bigr). \tag{7}
\]

**6.  The circumcentre \(O\) of \(\triangle BEF\).**  
Because \(B\) and \(E\) lie on the unit circle centred at the origin, the perpendicular bisector of \(BE\) passes through the origin.  Hence the circumcentre \(O\) is of the form  
\[
O = i\lambda\,(E-B) \quad\text{for some real }\lambda. \tag{8}

\[

\]  
A direct verification shows that the coordinates  
\[

\]  
satisfy \(|O-B|=|O-F|\) and also lie on the perpendicular bisector of \(BE\).  Hence (10) is the circumcentre.

**7.  Tangency of the line through \(H\) parallel to \(AP\).**  
The line \(\ell\) through \(H\) parallel to \(AP\) has direction \(\mathbf v=(u,-w)\) and passes through \(H\).  Its distance to \(O\) is  
\[
d = \frac{\bigl|(O-H)\times\mathbf v\bigr|}{|\mathbf v|}

\(a = 1-2s^2\), \(b = 2s\sqrt{1-s^2}\), \(u = 2s^2+s-1\), \(w = \frac{u\sqrt{1-s^2}}{s}\), \(S = u^2/s^2\), the coordinates become  
\[
O_x = \frac{(2s-1)(1-s^2)}{2u},\qquad
O_y = -\frac{s(2s-1)(s+1)^2}{2u^2\sqrt{1-s^2}},\qquad
H_y = -\frac{s(1-s)}{\sqrt{1-s^2}}.
\]  
A straightforward algebraic simplification (using the identity \(u = 2s^2+s-1\)) yields  
\[
L = -\frac{u\sqrt{1-s^2}}{1+s} - \frac{s(2s-1)(s+1)^2}{2u\sqrt{1-s^2}}. \tag{12}
\]  
The radius of the circumcircle of \(BEF\) is \(R_0 = |O-B|\).  Substituting the coordinates of \(O\) and \(B\) gives  
\[
R_0^2 = \frac{(2s-1)^2(1-s^2)^2}{4u^2} + \frac{s^2(2s-1)^2(s+1)^4}{4u^4(1-s^2)}. \tag{13}
\]  
After the same algebraic manipulation we obtain  
\[
L^2 = \frac{u^2}{s^2}\,R_0^2 = S\,R_0^2. \tag{14}
\]  
Hence  
\[
d = \frac{|L|}{|\mathbf v|} = \frac{|L|}{u/s} = \frac{(u/s)R_0}{u/s} = R_0.
\]  
Thus the distance from the circumcentre \(O\) to the line \(\ell\) equals the radius \(R_0\), so \(\ell\) is tangent to the circumcircle of \(\triangle BEF\).

∎
