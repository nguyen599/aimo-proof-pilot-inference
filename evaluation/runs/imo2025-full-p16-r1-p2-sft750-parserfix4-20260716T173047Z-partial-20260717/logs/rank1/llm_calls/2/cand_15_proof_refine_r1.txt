The altitude from \(P\) is vertical, so \(H\) lies on the line \(x=x_P\).  
The altitude from \(M\) is perpendicular to \(PN\); a direction vector is \((y_P,\,d-x_P)\).  
Intersecting with \(x=x_P\) gives  

\[
H = \Bigl(x_P,\; \frac{x_P(d-x_P)}{y_P}\Bigr). \tag{5}
\]

The line through \(H\) parallel to \(AP\) has direction \(\mathbf v\); we denote it by \(\ell\).

We shall prove that \(\ell\) is tangent to the circumcircle of \(\triangle BEF\).  
Let \(O\) be the circumcenter of \(\triangle BEF\).  Since \(E\) and \(F\) lie on the line through \(A\) with direction \(\mathbf v\), the midpoint of \(EF\) is \(A+\alpha\mathbf v\).  
The perpendicular bisector of \(EF\) is the line through this midpoint perpendicular to \(\mathbf v\); therefore we can write  

\[
O = A + \alpha\mathbf v + k\,\mathbf n,
\]

where \(\mathbf n\) is any vector perpendicular to \(\mathbf v\).  Choose  

\[
\mathbf n = (v_y,\,-v_x),
\]

so that \(\mathbf n\cdot\mathbf v=0\) and \(|\mathbf n|^2 = |\mathbf v|^2\).

The condition that \(O\) is equidistant from \(B\) and \(E\) gives  

\[
|O-B|^2 = |O-E|^2.
\]

Now \(O-B = (A-B)+\alpha\mathbf v + k\mathbf n\) and \(O-E = (\alpha-s)\mathbf v + k\mathbf n\).  
Because \(\mathbf v\cdot\mathbf n=0\), we obtain  

\[
|O-B|^2 = |(A-B)+\alpha\mathbf v|^2 + k^2|\mathbf v|^2 + 2k\,\mathbf n\cdot(A-B),
\qquad
|O-E|^2 = (\alpha-s)^2|\mathbf v|^2 + k^2|\mathbf v|^2.
\]

Equating and solving for \(k\) yields  

\[
k = \frac{(\alpha-s)^2|\mathbf v|^2 - |(A-B)+\alpha\mathbf v|^2}{2\,\mathbf n\cdot(A-B)}. \tag{6}
\]

Since \(A-B = (0,\,2y_A)\), we have \(\mathbf n\cdot(A-B) = -2v_x y_A\).  Hence  

\[
k = \frac{(\alpha-s)^2|\mathbf v|^2 - |(A-B)+\alpha\mathbf v|^2}{-4v_x y_A}. \tag{7}
\]

The circumradius of \(\triangle BEF\) is  

\[
R_c = |OB| = \sqrt{(\alpha-s)^2|\mathbf v|^2 + k^2|\mathbf v|^2}. \tag{8}
\]

Now we compute the distance from \(O\) to the line \(\ell\).  The normal vector to \(\ell\) is \(\mathbf n\); therefore  

\[
\operatorname{dist}(O,\ell) = \frac{|\mathbf n\cdot(O-H)|}{|\mathbf n|}.
\]

Using \(O = A + \alpha\mathbf v + k\mathbf n\) and \(H = (x_P,\,y_H)\), we have  

\[
O-H = (A-H) + \alpha\mathbf v + k\mathbf n.
\]

Because \(\mathbf n\cdot\mathbf v=0\),  

\[
\mathbf n\cdot(O-H) = \mathbf n\cdot(A-H) + k|\mathbf n|^2 = \mathbf n\cdot(A-H) + k|\mathbf v|^2.
\]

Now \(A-H = (x_A-x_P,\; y_A - y_H)\) and \(\mathbf n=(v_y,\,-v_x)\).  Using \(v_x = x_P-x_A\) we find  

\[
\mathbf n\cdot(A-H) = v_y(x_A-x_P) - v_x(y_A - y_H) = -v_x v_y - v_x y_A + v_x y_H = v_x(y_H - y_P),
\]

since \(v_y = y_P-y_A\).  Thus  

\[
\mathbf n\cdot(O-H) = v_x(y_H - y_P) + k|\mathbf v|^2. \tag{9}
\]

Consequently  

\[
\operatorname{dist}(O,\ell)^2 = \frac{\bigl(v_x(y_H - y_P) + k|\mathbf v|^2\bigr)^2}{|\mathbf v|^2}. \tag{10}
\]

We need to show that this distance equals the circumradius \(R_c\) given by (8).  Squaring (8) and equating with (10) leads to  

\[
\bigl(v_x(y_H - y_P) + k|\mathbf v|^2\bigr)^2 = |\mathbf v|^4\bigl((\alpha-s)^2 + k^2\bigr). \tag{11}
\]

Because both sides are non‑negative, (11) is equivalent to  

\[
\bigl(v_x(y_H - y_P) + k|\mathbf v|^2\bigr)^2 = |\mathbf v|^4(\alpha-s)^2 + |\mathbf v|^4 k^2.

Dividing by \(|\mathbf v|^2\) (which is positive) we obtain  

\[

Let \(T = \dfrac{v_x(y_H - y_P)}{|\mathbf v|^2}\).  Then (12) becomes
