Alice and Bazza are playing the inekoalaty game, a two-player game whose rules depend on a positive real number $\lambda$ which is known to both players. On the $n$th turn of the game (starting with $n=1$) the following happens:
\begin{itemize}

\[

\[

\]
\end{itemize}
If a player cannot choose a suitable $x_n$, the game ends and the other player wins. If the game goes on forever, neither player wins. All chosen numbers are known to both players.
Determine all values of $\lambda$ for which Alice has a winning strategy and all those for which Bazza has a winning strategy.

## Solution
Let the sum of chosen numbers after \(n\) turns be \(A_n\) and the sum of squares be \(S_n\). The rules are:

- For odd \(n\), Alice chooses \(x_n\ge0\) with \(A_n\le \lambda n\).
- For even \(n\), Bazza chooses \(x_n\ge0\) with \(S_n\le n\).

If a player cannot choose a nonnegative number satisfying the constraint, the other player wins. The game is infinite if both can always move.

We analyze the game by considering the state after each turn.

**1. Preliminaries.**  

Alice wins if at the end of some odd turn \(n\) we have \(S_n>n+1\) (because then at the beginning of the next turn (even \(n+1\)) Bazza cannot move). Bazza wins if at the end of some even turn \(n\) we have \(A_n>\lambda (n+1)\) (because then at the beginning of the next turn (odd \(n+2\)) Alice cannot move).

We will show that the threshold is \(\lambda=1/\sqrt2\).

**2. The case \(\lambda>1/\sqrt2\).**
