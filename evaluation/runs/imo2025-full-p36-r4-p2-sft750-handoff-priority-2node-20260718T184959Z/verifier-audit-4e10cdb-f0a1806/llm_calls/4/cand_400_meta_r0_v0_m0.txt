A proper divisor of a positive integer $N$ is a positive divisor of $N$ other than $N$ itself.
The infinite sequence $a_1, a_2, \cdots$ consists of positive integers, each of which has at least three proper divisors. For each $n \geqslant 1$, the integer $a_{n+1}$ is the sum of the three largest proper divisors of $a_n$.
Determine all possible values of $a_1$.

## Solution
We denote by \(f(N)\) the sum of the three largest proper divisors of a positive integer \(N\). The sequence is defined by \(a_{n+1}=f(a_n)\) and we require that each term has at least three proper divisors.

**1.  Formula for \(f(N)\).**

\[
d_{k-3}=\frac{N}{d_4},\quad d_{k-2}=\frac{N}{d_3},\quad d_{k-1}=\frac{N}{d_2}.
\]  
Hence  
\[
f(N)=\frac{N}{d_2}+\frac{N}{d_3}+\frac{N}{d_4}.
\]
