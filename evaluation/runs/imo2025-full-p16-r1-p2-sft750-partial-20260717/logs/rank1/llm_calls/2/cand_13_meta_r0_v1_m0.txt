Let $\Omega$ and $\Gamma$ be circles with centres $M$ and $N$, respectively, such that the radius of $\Omega$ is less than the radius of $\Gamma$. ## Solution

\[
r^2 = h^2+k^2,\qquad R^2 = (1-h)^2+k^2.
\]
Then \(B=(h,-k)\). The line \(MN\) is the \(x\)-axis, so the intersections with \(\Omega\) and \(\Gamma\) are
\[
C=(-r,0),\qquad D=(1+R,0).
\]
The circumcenter \(P\) of \(\triangle ACD\) satisfies \(x_P=\frac{1+R-r}{2}\) and from \(PA=PC\) we obtain
\[
y_P = -x_P\frac{h+r}{k}.

Let \(S=1+r+R\). Then \(\Delta x = \frac{(R-r)S}{2}\) and \(\Delta y = -\frac{(h+r)S(R-r+1)}{2k}\).

\[
2t(A\cdot u)+t^2|u|^2=0\;\Longrightarrow\; t=-\frac{2(A\cdot u)}{|u|^2}.
\]

\[
E = A+\lambda u,\quad \lambda = \frac{rS}{|u|^2}.
\]
Similarly, using \((A-1)\cdot u\) we obtain
\[
F = A+\mu u,\quad \mu = \frac{RS}{|u|^2}.
\]
Direct substitution of the expressions for \(\Delta x,\Delta y\) and the relations among \(h,k,r,R\) shows
\[
A\cdot u = -\frac{rS}{2},\qquad (A-1)\cdot u = -\frac{RS}{2}.
\]
Hence \(\lambda,\mu\) as above.

Now consider the circumcircle of \(\triangle BEF\). Its center \(O=(a,b)\) satisfies
\[
|O-B|^2=|O-E|^2,\quad |O-B|^2=|O-F|^2.
\]
Subtracting the two equalities gives
\[
2O\cdot(F-E)=|F|^2-|E|^2.
\]

2O\cdot(E-B)=|E|^2-|B|^2.
\]

O\cdot(\lambda\Delta x,\,2k+\lambda\Delta y)=\lambda A\cdot u+\frac{\lambda^2|u|^2}{2}. \tag{2}

H=(x_P,\,y_H),\qquad y_H=\frac{x_P(1-x_P)}{y_P}.

\[
y_H=-\frac{k}{S}.
\]
Thus \(H=\bigl(x_P,\,-\frac{k}{S}\bigr)\).

Now consider the line \(L\) through \(H\) parallel to \(AP\); it has direction \(u=(\Delta x,\Delta y)\). The distance from \(O\) to \(L\) is
\[
d(O,L)=\frac{|(O-H)\times u|}{|u|}.
\]

R_c^2 = \Bigl(\frac12-h\Bigr)^2+(b+k)^2.

From the relations for \(h\) and \(k\) we have \(\frac12-h=\frac{R^2-r^2}{2}=\frac{(R-r)(R+r)}{2}\). Also \(b+k = k\Bigl(1-\frac{R+r}{2X}\Bigr)\).
