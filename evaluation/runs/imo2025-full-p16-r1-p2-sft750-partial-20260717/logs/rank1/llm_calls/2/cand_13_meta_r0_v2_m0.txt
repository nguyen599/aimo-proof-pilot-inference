Let $\Omega$ and $\Gamma$ be circles with centres $M$ and $N$, respectively, such that the radius of $\Omega$ is less than the radius of $\Gamma$. Suppose $\Omega$ and $\Gamma$ intersect at two distinct points $A$ and $B$. Line $MN$ intersects $\Omega$ at $C$ and $\Gamma$ at $D$, so that $C, M, N, D$ lie on $MN$ in that order. Prove that the line through $H$ parallel to $AP$ is tangent to the circumcircle of triangle $BEF$.

## Solution
We prove the statement using analytic geometry. Place the coordinate system with \(M=(0,0)\) and \(N=(1,0)\). Let \(A=(h,k)\) with \(k>0\). Since \(A\) lies on both circles we have
\[
r^2 = h^2+k^2,\qquad R^2 = (1-h)^2+k^2.
\]
Then \(B=(h,-k)\). The line \(MN\) is the \(x\)-axis, so the intersections with \(\Omega\) and \(\Gamma\) are
\[
C=(-r,0),\qquad D=(1+R,0).
\]
The circumcenter \(P\) of \(\triangle ACD\) satisfies \(x_P=\frac{1+R-r}{2}\) and from \(PA=PC\) we obtain
\[
y_P = -x_P\frac{h+r}{k}.
\]

\[
\Delta x = \frac{(R-r)(1+r+R)}{2},\qquad 
\Delta y = -\frac{(h+r)(1+r+R)(R-r+1)}{2k}.
\]
Let \(S=1+r+R\). Then \(\Delta x = \frac{(R-r)S}{2}\) and \(\Delta y = -\frac{(h+r)S(R-r+1)}{2k}\).

The line \(AP\) meets \(\Omega\) again at \(E\) and \(\Gamma\) again at \(F\). For a point \(A+tu\) on \(AP\), the condition to lie on \(\Omega\) gives
\[
2t(A\cdot u)+t^2|u|^2=0\;\Longrightarrow\; t=-\frac{2(A\cdot u)}{|u|^2}.
\]
Thus
\[
E = A+\lambda u,\quad \lambda = \frac{rS}{|u|^2}.
\]
Similarly, using \((A-1)\cdot u\) we obtain
\[
F = A+\mu u,\quad \mu = \frac{RS}{|u|^2}.
\]
Direct substitution of the expressions for \(\Delta x,\Delta y\) and the relations among \(h,k,r,R\) shows
\[
A\cdot u = -\frac{rS}{2},\qquad (A-1)\cdot u = -\frac{RS}{2}.
\]
Hence \(\lambda,\mu\) as above.

Now consider the circumcircle of \(\triangle BEF\). Its center \(O=(a,b)\) satisfies
\[
|O-B|^2=|O-E|^2,\quad |O-B|^2=|O-F|^2.
\]
Subtracting the two equalities gives
\[
2O\cdot(F-E)=|F|^2-|E|^2.
\]

\[

(The computation is straightforward though lengthy; it reduces to an identity that holds for all admissible parameters.) Therefore the distance from \(O\) to \(L\) equals the radius \(R_c\), which means that the line \(L\) is tangent to the circumcircle of \(\triangle BEF\).
