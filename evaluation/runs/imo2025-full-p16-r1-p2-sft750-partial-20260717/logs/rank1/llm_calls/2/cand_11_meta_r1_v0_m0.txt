Let \(A=(a,b)\) with \(b>0\).  
Since the circles intersect, the second intersection point is the reflection of \(A\) across \(MN\); hence \(B=(a,-b)\).  

The radii are  
\[
r^2 = a^2+b^2,\qquad R^2 = (a-2)^2+b^2.
\]  
Because the circles are distinct and \(r<R\) we have \(a<1\).  

The line \(MN\) meets \(\Omega\) at \(C\) and \(\Gamma\) at \(D\) in the order \(C,M,N,D\).  
Thus

### 1.  Circumcenter \(P\) of \(\triangle ACD\)

The perpendicular bisector of \(CD\) is vertical through the midpoint of \(CD\); its equation is  
\[

### 2.  Orthocenter \(H\) of \(\triangle PMN\)

### 3.  The points \(E\) and \(F\)
