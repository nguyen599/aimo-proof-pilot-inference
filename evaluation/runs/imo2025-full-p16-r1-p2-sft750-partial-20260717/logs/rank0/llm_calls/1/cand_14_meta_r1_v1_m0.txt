We determine all nonnegative integers $k$ for which there exist $n$ distinct lines covering $T$ (i.e., every point of $T$ lies on at least one of the lines) and exactly $k$ of them are sunny.

---

### 1.  Existence of constructions

* **$k=0$:** Take the $n$ vertical lines $x=1,\,2,\dots,n$.  Each point $(a,b)\in T$ lies on the line $x=a$, so all points are covered.  These lines are all non‑sunny (vertical), so $k=0$ works.

* **$k=3$:** For $n\ge 3$, take the $n-3$ lines $x+y = 4,\;5,\;\dots,\;n+1$ (these are non‑sunny) and the three sunny lines
