\bigl(\lambda(2k-1)-(k-1)\sqrt2\bigr)^2 > 2.
\]
Let \(K\) be the smallest such \(k\). Because \(\lambda>\sqrt2/2\) we have \(2\lambda-\sqrt2>0\), so
\[
a_k = \lambda(2k-1)-(k-1)\sqrt2 = (2\lambda-\sqrt2)k+(\sqrt2-\lambda)\xrightarrow[k\to\infty]{}\infty.
\]
Hence such a \(K\) exists. Up to turn \(2K-3\) she has chosen \(0\), and Bazza’s optimal response (to minimise her slack) is to choose the maximal allowed numbers, so that
\[
S_{2k-2}=k\sqrt2,\quad Q_{2k-2}=2k-2
\]
for all \(k\) (the inequalities hold with equality because the maximal choices are used). Thus at the start of her \(K\)-th odd turn we have
\[
S_{2K-2}=(K-1)\sqrt2,\quad Q_{2K-2}=2K-2.
\]
Her slack is
\[
\lambda(2K-1)-S_{2K-2} = \lambda(2K-1)-(K-1)\sqrt2 > \sqrt2.
\]
She chooses \(x_{2K-1} = \lambda(2K-1)-S_{2K-2}\) (the maximum allowed). Then
\[
Q_{2K-1}=Q_{2K-2}+x_{2K-1}^2 = 2K-2 + \bigl(\lambda(2K-1)-(K-1)\sqrt2\bigr)^2 > 2K.
\]
Now Bazza on his turn \(2K\) must choose \(x_{2K}\ge0\) with \(Q_{2K}\le 2K\), but already \(Q_{2K-1}>2K\), so even \(x_{2K}=0\) would violate the condition. Therefore Bazza cannot move and Alice wins.

If Bazza ever chooses a smaller number than the maximum, the sum \(S\) becomes even smaller, which only increases Alice’s slack and makes her win even easier. Thus this is a winning strategy for Alice.

---

### 3.  The case \(\lambda=\sqrt2/2\)

We show that neither player can force a win; the game can be played indefinitely. Let Alice always choose \(x_{2k-1}=0\) and let Bazza always choose the maximal allowed numbers. Then as above we have \(S_{2k}=k\sqrt2\) and \(Q_{2k}=2k\). For each odd turn \(2k-1\) the slack is
\[
\lambda(2k-1)-S_{2k-2} = \frac{\sqrt2}{2}(2k-1)-(k-1)\sqrt2 = \frac{\sqrt2}{2}.
\]
Hence the maximum possible \(x_{2k-1}\) is \(\sqrt2/2\), and
\[
Q_{2k-1}+(\sqrt2/2)^2 = 2k-2 + \frac12 = 2k-1.5 < 2k,
\]
so Alice cannot win by playing the maximum. Moreover
\[
S_{2k}=k\sqrt2 \le \frac{\sqrt2}{2}(2k+1)=k\sqrt2+\frac{\sqrt2}{2}
\]
holds for every \(k\), so Alice can always move. The game therefore continues forever, and neither player has a winning strategy.

---

### 4.  Conclusion

- Alice has a winning strategy if and only if \(\lambda > \dfrac{\sqrt2}{2}\).
- Bazza has a winning strategy if and only if \(\lambda < \dfrac{\sqrt2}{2}\).
- For \(\lambda = \dfrac{\sqrt2}{2}\) neither player has a winning strategy (the game can be played indefinitely).

\[
\boxed{\text{Alice wins for }\lambda>\frac{\sqrt2}{2},\quad\text{Bazza wins for }\lambda<\frac{\sqrt2}{2},\quad\text{draw for }\lambda=\frac{\sqrt2}{2}}
\]
</solution>
