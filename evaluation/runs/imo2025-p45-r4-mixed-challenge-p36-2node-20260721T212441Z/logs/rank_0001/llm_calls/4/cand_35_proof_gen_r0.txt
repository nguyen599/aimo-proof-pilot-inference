Let \(f(N)\) denote the sum of the three largest proper divisors of \(N\).  
For a composite integer \(N\) with at least three proper divisors we can list all positive divisors of \(N\) in increasing order: \(1 = d_1 < d_2 < \dots < d_k = N\).  
The proper divisors are \(d_2, d_3, \dots, d_{k-1}\).  
The three largest proper divisors correspond to \(d_{k-2}, d_{k-1}, d_k\) but the ordering of the divisors is reversed when we consider the complementary divisors: if \(r = N/d\) then \(r\) is an integer and the map \(d \mapsto r\) is order‑reversing.  
Hence the three largest proper divisors are \(N/r_1, N/r_2, N/r_3\) where \(r_1 < r_2 < r_3\) are the three smallest divisors of \(N\) greater than \(1\).  
Thus  
\[
f(N) = \frac{N}{r_1} + \frac{N}{r_2} + \frac{N}{r_3}.
\]
