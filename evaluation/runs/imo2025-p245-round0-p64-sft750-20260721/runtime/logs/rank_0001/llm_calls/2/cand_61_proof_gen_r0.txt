Because $d-p = \frac{d-R+r}{2}$, we have

\[
\lambda_F = \frac{R(d+r+R)}{|v|^2}.

\[

\[
O = \Bigl(\frac{d}{2},\; \frac{y}{2}+\frac{d-R+r}{4s}\Bigr).
\]
One verifies that $|O-B| = |O-E| = |O-F|$; hence $O$ is the circumcenter of $\triangle BEF$.

We now prove that the line through $H$ parallel to $AP$ is tangent to the circumcircle of $\triangle BEF$. Let $L$ be this line. Its direction vector is $v$, and it passes through $H$; therefore $L$ has equation $(x-p,\; y-y_H)$ parallel to $v$. The distance from $O$ to $L$ is
\[
\operatorname{dist}(O,L) = \frac{|\det(O-H,\;v)|}{|v|}.
\]

Compute $O-H = \bigl(\frac{d}{2}-p,\; Y-y_H\bigr)$ where $Y = \frac{y}{2}+\frac{d-R+r}{4s}$. \[
\bigl(O-H\bigr)\times v = \pm |v|\cdot|O-B|.
\]
Hence $\operatorname{dist}(O,L) = |O-B|$, which is exactly the radius of the circle $(BEF)$. Therefore $L$ is tangent to the circumcircle of $\triangle BEF$. ∎
