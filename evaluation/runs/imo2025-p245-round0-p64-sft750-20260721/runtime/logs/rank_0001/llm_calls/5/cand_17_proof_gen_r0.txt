Since \(S_{2k}>\lambda(2k+1)\), even \(x_{2k+1}=0\) violates the constraint, so no legal move exists. Alice loses, and Bazza wins. If Alice ever chooses a positive number on her turn, \(S\) becomes even larger, making the loss inevitable. Therefore Bazza has a winning strategy for every \(\lambda<\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\).

---

### 3. \(\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\le\lambda\le1\): neither player has a winning strategy (draw).

Both players can simply always choose \(0\). Then \(S_n=0\le\lambda n\) and \(Q_n=0\le n\) for every \(n\); all constraints are satisfied and the game continues forever. Hence a drawing strategy exists for both players, and consequently neither can force a win. The game is a draw for these \(\lambda\).

---

### Conclusion
- Alice has a winning strategy iff \(\lambda>1\).
- Bazza has a winning strategy iff \(\lambda<\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\).
- For \(\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\le\lambda\le1\), neither player has a winning strategy (the game can be drawn).

\[
\boxed{\text{Alice wins for }\lambda>1;\quad \text{Bazza wins for }\lambda<\frac{1}{\sqrt2};\quad \text{for }\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\le\lambda\le1\text{ the game is drawn.}}
\]
