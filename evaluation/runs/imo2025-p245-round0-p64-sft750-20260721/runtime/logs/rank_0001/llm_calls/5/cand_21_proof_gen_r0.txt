b_k = \sqrt{2k - \bigl(\sum_{i=1}^{k} a_i^2 + \sum_{i=1}^{k-1} b_i^2\bigr)}.
\]
This is always legal because the sum of squares after his move becomes exactly \(2k\), which satisfies the condition.

We show by induction that after each of Bazza's moves the total sum \(S_{2k} = \sum_{i=1}^{2k} x_i\) satisfies \(S_{2k} \ge \sqrt{2}\,k\).  
Base \(k=1\): \(S_2 = a_1+b_1\), with \(b_1 = \sqrt{2-a_1^2}\). The function \(f(a)=a+\sqrt{2-a^2}\) on \([0,\sqrt{2}]\) attains its minimum \(\sqrt{2}\) at the endpoints, so \(S_2 \ge \sqrt{2}\).  

Inductive step: assume \(S_{2k} \ge \sqrt{2}\,k\) after Bazza's \(k\)-th move. Alice then chooses \(a_{k+1} \ge 0\) such that \(A_{k+1}+B_k \le \lambda(2k+1)\), where \(A_{k+1}=A_k+a_{k+1}\) and \(B_k\) is the sum of Bazza's previous choices. Bazza's subsequent move gives \(b_{k+1} = \sqrt{2 - a_{k+1}^2}\) (because \(Q_{2k+1}=2k+a_{k+1}^2\) and he picks the maximum allowed). Hence
\[
S_{2(k+1)} = S_{2k} + a_{k+1} + \sqrt{2-a_{k+1}^2} \ge S_{2k} + \sqrt{2} \ge \sqrt{2}\,k + \sqrt{2} = \sqrt{2}\,(k+1).
\]
Thus the induction holds.

Now choose \(k\) large enough so that \(\sqrt{2}\,k > \lambda(2k+1)\). Because \(\lambda < \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\), we have \(2\lambda < \sqrt{2}\), so \((2\lambda - \sqrt{2})k + \lambda\) is negative for \(k > \frac{\lambda}{\sqrt{2}-2\lambda}\). For such \(k\), we have \(\sqrt{2}\,k > \lambda(2k+1)\). Since \(S_{2k} \ge \sqrt{2}\,k\), it follows that \(S_{2k} > \lambda(2k+1)\). At the start of Alice's turn \(n=2k+1\), she must choose \(a_{k+1} \ge 0\) so that \(S_{2k}+a_{k+1} \le \lambda(2k+1)\). But \(S_{2k} > \lambda(2k+1)\) already, so even \(a_{k+1}=0\) violates the inequality; thus she cannot move and loses. Therefore Bazza wins.

**3. For \(\lambda \ge \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\), Bazza has no winning strategy.**  
Alice can survive indefinitely by always choosing \(x_n = 0\).  
At her turn \(n=2k+1\) she needs \(S_{2k} \le \lambda(2k+1)\). With her playing \(0\) we have \(\sum a_i = 0\), so \(S_{2k} = \sum b_i\). Bazza's move gives \(\sum b_i^2 = 2k\). By Cauchy–Schwarz,
\[
S_{2k} = \sum_{i=1}^{k} b_i \le \sqrt{k \cdot \sum_{i=1}^{k} b_i^2} = \sqrt{k \cdot 2k} = \sqrt{2}\,k.
\]
For \(\lambda \ge \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\) we obtain
\[
\lambda(2k+1) \ge \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}(2k+1) = \sqrt{2}\,k + \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} > \sqrt{2}\,k \ge S_{2k},
\]
so the condition holds for every \(k\). Hence Alice can always choose \(0\) and the game never ends; thus Bazza cannot force a win.

**Conclusion.**  
- Alice has no winning strategy for any \(\lambda > 0\).  
- Bazza has a winning strategy if and only if \(0 < \lambda < \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\); for \(\lambda \ge \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\) the game can be drawn (neither player can force a win).
</solution>
