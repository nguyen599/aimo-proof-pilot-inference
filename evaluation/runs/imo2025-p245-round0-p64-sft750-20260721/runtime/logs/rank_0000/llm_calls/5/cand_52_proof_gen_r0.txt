Assume \(S_{2k-2}\ge (k-1)\sqrt2\). Then  
\[
S_{2k}=S_{2k-2}+x_{2k-1}+\sqrt{2k-Q_{2k-1}} = S_{2k-2}+x_{2k-1}+\sqrt{2-x_{2k-1}^2}
\]  
because \(Q_{2k-1}=2k-2+x_{2k-1}^2\).  
The function \(f(t)=t+\sqrt{2-t^2}\) satisfies \(f(t)\ge\sqrt2\) for every \(t\in[0,\sqrt2]\) (its minimum is \(\sqrt2\) at the endpoints). Hence  
\[
x_{2k-1}+\sqrt{2-x_{2k-1}^2} \ge \sqrt2 .
\]  
Therefore \(S_{2k}\ge S_{2k-2}+\sqrt2 \ge (k-1)\sqrt2+\sqrt2 = k\sqrt2\).  
By induction \(S_{2k}\ge k\sqrt2\) for all \(k\).

Now we show that for sufficiently large \(k\) we have \(S_{2k} > \lambda(2k+1)\).  
Since \(\lambda\le\frac23\), we have \(\lambda(2k+1)\le\frac23(2k+1)=\frac{4k}{3}+\frac23\).  
We need \(k\sqrt2 > \frac{4k}{3}+\frac23\), i.e. \(k(\sqrt2-\frac43) > \frac23\).  
Because \(\sqrt2-\frac43 \approx 0.08088 >0\), this inequality holds for all \(k\) larger than \(\frac{2/3}{\sqrt2-4/3}\approx8.236\).  
In particular, for \(k=9\) we get \(9\sqrt2 \approx12.727 > \frac{2}{3}\cdot19 = \frac{38}{3}\approx12.667\).  
Thus \(S_{18} \ge 9\sqrt2 > \lambda\cdot19\).  
After Bazza's move at \(n=18\) we have \(S_{18} > \lambda\cdot19\).  
Now Alice's turn at \(n=19\): she must choose \(x_{19}\ge0\) so that \(S_{19}\le\lambda\cdot19\).  
But \(S_{19}=S_{18}+x_{19}\ge S_{18} > \lambda\cdot19\), so no such \(x_{19}\) exists. Hence Alice cannot move and Bazza wins.

Therefore Bazza has a winning strategy for every \(\lambda\le\frac23\).

---
### 3.  Conclusion

Alice has a winning strategy if and only if \(\lambda > \frac23\);  
Bazza has a winning strategy if and only if \(\lambda \le \frac23\).  
(For \(\lambda = \frac23\) the game ends with Bazza's win.)
</solution>
