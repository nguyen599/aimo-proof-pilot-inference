If $k\ge1$, then $a_1$ is divisible by $12$ and $f(a_1)=\frac{13}{12}a_1=2^{2k-1}\cdot3^{m-1}\cdot13\cdot t$.  Repeating, after $k$ steps we obtain
\[
N_k=2^{1}\cdot3^{m-k}\cdot13^{k}\cdot t.
\]
Because $m\ge k+1$, we have $m-k\ge1$.  This number has $v_2=1$, $v_3\ge1$, and no factor $5$ (since $t$ has no $5$ and $13$ is not $5$).  Hence for such a number the three smallest divisors are $2,3,6$ and $f(N_k)=N_k$.  Therefore the sequence becomes constant from that point onward, and every term has at least three proper divisors.

Thus the conditions are sufficient.

---

### 6.  Conclusion

The possible values of $a_1$ are exactly the positive integers $N$ such that
\[
5\nmid N,\quad v_2(N)\text{ is odd},\quad v_3(N)\ge\frac{v_2(N)+1}{2}.
\]
Equivalently, $N$ can be written as
\[
N=2^{2k+1}\cdot3^{m}\cdot t,
\]
with $k\ge0$, $m\ge k+1$, and $t$ an integer coprime to $6$ and not divisible by $5$.
