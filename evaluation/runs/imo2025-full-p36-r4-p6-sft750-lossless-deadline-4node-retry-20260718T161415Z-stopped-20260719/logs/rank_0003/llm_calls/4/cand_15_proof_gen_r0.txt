If $a_1$ is even but $3\nmid a_1$, Lemma 2 gives $a_2=f(a_1)<a_1$. Also $a_2$ is even? Not necessarily, but the sequence is strictly decreasing (since $f(N)<N$ for such $N$). Consequently, for an infinite sequence we must have $a_1$ even and divisible by $3$.
